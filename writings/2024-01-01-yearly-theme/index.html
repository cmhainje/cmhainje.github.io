2024, Year of the Garden.

Published 2024 January 07.

In 2024, I want to treat my life a bit more like a garden, of which I am merely a steward. For one, my life is made up of many things that require regular tending to, like my physical and mental health, my relationships, my creative output. More, though, I'm coming to understand that time is something like this as well.

I believe that there really is no such thing as "time" for a person---at least, not in the sense that it's a resource to be quantified or appraised. Instead, we have lives; time is just the medium on which our lives are lived out. A "chunk of time" is really a chunk of our life. And from this perspective, whether something is "worth" your time takes a drastically different meaning. As we fill our lives with things that we (hopefully) find meaningful---work, relationships, hobbies---each of these becomes a draw on our time, or, perhaps a better way to think of it, a choice about how we live. Without regular tending, I think it can be easy for things which aren't particularly meaningful to overrun the rest of the garden and have an outsized influence on the way we live.

My response to this is to try to be a gardener with my time and life. To choose carefully what I allow into my garden, recognizing that there is not infinite space. To regularly re-evaluate and prune it, preventing the overgrowth of unnecessary or unfulfilling draws on my time. To recognize that each thing takes its own amount of time to grow, and that rushing it can only be harmful. To give to each the proper amount of energy; too much or too little energy on any one thing can only destabilize the garden.

Thus, 2024 is the Year of the Garden. In the rest of this post, I'll try to detail a little more concretely the most important goals and subthemes contained herein.
