Fraction direct

One of our metrics for understanding the blob as it relates to track geometry has been plotting the fraction of detected photons which were emitted with p_z < 0. A week or two ago, I found that this fraction is high for low z, and low for high z (as we would expect), with a rather smooth gradient between the two extremes. In the range of the blob, however, there is no gradient, but the fraction holds flat at about 0.5. This was before we implemented cleaning cuts, however, so we were a bit concerned that this feature may have been caused by some of the photons that we clean with our cuts.
To find out, I remade the plot but with our new 10M events' worth of data across the entire bar. Here is the plot:

Compared to before, the gradient is even more extreme from high to low. In fact, it appears that for nearly two-thirds of the bar, we do not detect photons emitted with p_z < 0; only those which reflect off the mirror appear to make it to the PMTs. This is surprising, but some findings that Alex showed in our meeting.
